Research

  • Jammer Source Localization with Federated Learning

    We develop a federated learning framework for privacy-preserving GNSS jammer localization from crowdsourced received signal strength (RSS) measurements. By augmenting physics-based models with neural networks, we achieve robust localization and field prediction even in complex urban environments affected by multipath and shadowing. Our work uses decentralized, privacy-preserving optimization, enables unsupervised localization of jammer sources, and incorporates uncertainty modeling — setting a new benchmark for GNSS security in crowdsourced environments.

Predicted jammer field and source localization in an urban scenario using the Augmented Physics-Based Model (APBM).
